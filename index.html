How it works

Four steps.
Then it's running.

Setup happens once, entirely in Telegram. After that, Rhino works — you supervise.

01

Choose or build a persona

Pick a preset temperament and thesis, or write your own from a blank prompt and a set of risk rules.

/persona set analyst
→ persona locked
02

Fund the agent's card

Rhino gets its own dedicated card, tied to a dedicated account. It never sees the rest of your balance.

/fund 2500 usdc
→ card active · •••0402
03

Connect it to Telegram

Rhino becomes a bot in your chat — previews, fills, and rebalances land there as they happen.

/connect telegram
→ bot linked
04

Set the guardrails, then step back

Spend caps, position limits, approval thresholds. Rhino enforces them on every single trade.

/limit 300/day, kill:on
→ guardrails live

Under the hood

Speaks the chain's language.

Rhino connects to Robinhood's Trading and Banking infrastructure over MCP, and settles data and execution costs on-chain using x402 — the HTTP-native payment standard built for agents, not humans typing card numbers.

  • Every priced call returns an HTTP 402, gets paid in stablecoin, and resolves in seconds — no API keys held anywhere.
  • Trades settle on Robinhood Chain, so positions, cash, and history are yours, not locked inside a black box.
  • Every payment writes an immutable line to the audit log you can read, right in Telegram.

Trust, by design

Autonomous doesn't
mean unsupervised.

Rhino can act without you in the room. It can never act outside the fence you built around it.

01

Dedicated funded card

Rhino spends from its own card and account only — no visibility into the rest of your portfolio.

02

Hard spend limits

Daily and per-trade caps you set once. Rhino can't request more, can't override them.

03

One-tap kill switch

Disconnect the agent instantly from Telegram. Trading halts immediately.

04

Full audit trail

Every trade and payment settles on-chain and logs to your chat — what, when, and why.
